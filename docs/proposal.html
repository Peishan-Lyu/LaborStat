World Demographics Study – a Visualization Perspective

Chapter 2 Proposal

2.1 Research topic

The first objective of this project is to highlight the current situation and historical trends. This shows us where we are, and where we have come from. These are the aspects we want to present:

  • How have the world and regional populations evolved over the past 5 decades?
  • Which regions have the youngest / oldest population?
  • Which regions have had the highest / lowest birth rate, death rate, and population growth?
  • How have birth rates changed cross-sectionally by country?
  • How has world longevity evolved over the past decades?
  • Is there any gender asymmetry in the birth rate and longevity?

The second objective of this project is to look for patterns and relationships among demographic and other factors. Hopefully this can reveal some hidden and intrinsic logics, and provide us with more profound understandings of world demographics. These are the questions we want to answer via visualization:

  • How do other demographic factors affect birth rate?
  • How do non-demographic factors (economic, healthcare, and education) affect birth rate?
  • How does income category affect longevity?
  • How does income category affect death rates of difference causes?
  • How do labor force and quality affect economic development?

2.2 Data availability

To answer the questions in the previous section, we need periodic data in various demographic and non-demographic statistics, broken down into regions and countries. The data should cover fields such as: * Demographic: population by gender and age group, birth rate, death rate, life expectancy * Economic: gdp per capita, income per capita, economic growth * Social: education, medical services * Policies:

A reliable data source that serves our purposes is the World Bank’s database “Health Nutrition and Population Statistics” (https://databank.worldbank.org/source/health-nutrition-and-population-statistics). They maintain comprehensive demographic and non-demographic data covering the aspects mentioned above.

The data can be downloaded as spreadsheet from the interface, by specifying filters on country, series (field), and year. Data can be imported to RStudio from the spreadsheet.
